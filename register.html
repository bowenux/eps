About this Webinar

Join us as Julie guides us through S.P.I.R.E.® – an intensive, multisensory reading intervention program designed specifically for nonreaders and struggling readers.

SPIRE is now available in print and digital options for flexible implementation.

The webinar will provide an overview of the program and introduce some key differences between using S.P.I.R.E. print or the new online version called iSPIRE as a direct instruction model--such as using traditional print and manipulatives, compared to using an iPad or other device, to teach foundation skills in reading with this target population.

You will learn

  • The components of S.P.I.R.E., why they are effective, and how they appeal to students and teachers
  • The 10 steps that make up a S.P.I.R.E. lesson, including details and the importance of each
  • The instructional support your teachers will receive, both through the program, as well as from our Professional Services team

About the Speaker

Julie Ross

S.P.I.R.E., National Trainer

Certified Special Education teacher in the areas of Learning Disabilities, Behavioral Disorders, and Educable Mental Handicaps.

Julie has over 25 years of teaching experience with students at all education levels, K-12, in both private and public school settings. Since 2005, Julie has acted as a S.P.I.R.E. consultant, working closely with teachers in districts across the country as they learn and implement the S.P.I.R.E. reading intervention program.
